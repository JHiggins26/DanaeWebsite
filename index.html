About Vetta

Founder and CEO of Write It Out Publishing LLC est. 2017
Where are you from?
My mother went into labor in Arizona, December 23, 1990 and raised me in East Baltimore City.
When did you know you wanted to be an Author?
Well, let my mom tell it, when I was eleven years old, I wrote on a piece of paper that I was going to be an author and my books will go all around the world. She says that she still has that piece of paper until this day. However, I do not remember this memory. Writing has always been my scape goat. It didn’t matter what I was writing, it’ll just always relax me. My Dad says I would write my name everywhere, on the walls, on his leather seats, etc. My parents also made me do book reports that were due to them, but it was something I didn’t mind doing because if it involved a pen and paper I was down for it. With that being said, it wasn’t until high school when my mom put me into counseling and the counselor suggested that I should write down my feelings and experiences down. That’s when I started to keep a journal and the real life stories began. In 2011, I was working in an inbound call center that barely received inbound calls, so 90 percent of the time I wasn’t doing anything. We also couldn’t have pen and paper at the desk so I started to write my life in story form on paper towels and would transfer it to paper on breaks and at home, but as a girl named Sunny, Which is now a book titled “Sunny North to Sunny South.”
Where did your Author name come from?
My Dad gave me this name. He named me. Vetta has her own way of things and so does Shantell.
Not only are you an author, but you have a publishing company?
Yes, write it out publishing is a publishing company that I decided to create for the simple fact that I wanted to self-publish. In the mist of that, I started to grow more ideas and wanted to be a stepping stone and create opportunities for others as well that may want to write, illustrate, etc.
What will be Write It Out Publishing’s first book? And what is it about?
“Little Black Girl” is our first book that is published under our company. It’s a book of affirmations, positivity, and creation.
Is it a children’s book?
It is for all ages. I’ll like to say it’s a book for families that speaks on esoteric knowledge and spirituality.
Where did the inspiration come from?
I woke up at like three in the morning one night, it was an outer-body experience, and I say that because it was a blurr. Like a day or two later I remembered that I wrote something and read it. It was initially a poem and I was talking to my younger self and informing myself of the true history and what must be unlearned and relearned. I put it in Children’s book form because I wanted everyone including a six year old to understand and comprehend the message.
How would you explain the process to get to this point?
All I can say is, trust your process and learn to go with your first instinct. I would have bad anxiety and sometimes I still do and it will have me very indecisive, I would also get so nervous with the thought of releasing anything. But, with the help of meditation, meditation music, affirmations, positive thinking, practicing law of attraction, and all of my “you are confident speeches” to myself I was able to get to this point and I still have more fighting and learning to do.
Can we expect more from Author Vetta Shantell?
Yes, Little Black Boy, The Universal Goddess, Sunny North to Sunny South, Mood swings which is poetry, will all be coming out soon, plus more.
Is there anything you would like to add?
Yes, special shout out to my illustrator Alexis Nelson. She did the cover to Sunny North to Sunny South and she was able to bring Little Black Girl to life with the help of another amazing artist Jalia E. Also, a special shout-out to everyone who has supported me by giving me advice, encouraging words, motivation when I was ready to give up, those who let me cry on their shoulders while having an emotional break down, and those who’ve supported me by sharing my posts, buying books, and passing it on by word-of-mouth. I thank you all.
